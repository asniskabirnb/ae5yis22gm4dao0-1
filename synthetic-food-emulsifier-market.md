<p><p>The synthetic food emulsifier market includes several types, each serving unique functions in food formulation. Span and Tween are non-ionic emulsifiers that stabilize emulsions, with Span being more effective in water-in-oil systems and Tween in oil-in-water applications. Glyceryl monostearate, a widely used emulsifier, helps improve texture and shelf life in various foods. Additionally, other emulsifiers encompass a range of synthetic options that cater to specific dietary requirements and enhance food quality, texture, and consistency across products.</p></p>
