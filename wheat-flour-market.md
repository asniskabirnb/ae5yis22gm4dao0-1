<p><ul><li>Self Raising Flour</li><li>Bread Flour</li><li>Biscuit Flour</li><li>Cake Flour</li><li>Whole Meal Flour</li><li>Resultant Flour</li><li>Semolina</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The wheat flour market comprises various types catering to specific baking needs. Self-raising flour contains leavening agents, ideal for quick breads and pancakes. Bread flour has a higher protein content, perfect for yeast-based recipes. Biscuit flour is low-protein, creating tender biscuits. Cake flour is finely milled for light, airy cakes. Whole meal flour retains the bran and germ, offering nutritional benefits. Resultant flour, a byproduct of milling, is versatile. Semolina, coarsely ground durum wheat, is used for pasta and couscous.</p></p>
